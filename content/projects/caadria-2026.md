## Overview

Image-to-image models such as pix2pix are increasingly used to generate architectural floor plans, yet many evaluations emphasize visual similarity rather than spatial or geometric validity. This project develops a deterministic benchmark to test the practical performance limits of pix2pix in floor-plan generation.

The study uses a shape grammar to create paired datasets that map low-detail parti diagrams to furnished plans. By holding model settings and dataset structure steady, the benchmark makes it possible to observe where the model learns rule-consistent transformations and where it fails.

## Research Focus

- Pix2pix and architectural floor-plan generation
- Shape grammar as a deterministic dataset generator
- Rule consistency as an evaluation problem
- Benchmarking beyond visual fidelity
