## Overview

This research explores how rule-based architectural design can be connected to image-generation workflows. The project addresses a recurring limitation in AI-assisted design: renderings from different views often fail to describe the same spatial proposal consistently.

The workflow starts from a design parti, develops feasible floor-plan variations through shape grammar procedures, and then uses generated representations as structured inputs for Stable Diffusion. Rather than treating AI imagery as a detached visualization step, the project positions it inside a conceptual design workflow where grammar, drawing, and rendering inform one another.

## Research Focus

- Shape grammars for early-stage architectural generation
- Multi-view consistency in AI-generated renderings
- Stable Diffusion as part of a design workflow rather than only an image tool
- Computational links between parti, plan, three-dimensional form, and atmosphere
